Meat for the Harvest

Fighter Skills

True Grit: When making an Injury roll, roll 1 less Injury dice. If the fighter was rolling only 1 Injury die, roll two and the controlling player chooses which to discard.

Iron Jaw: Increase this fighter's Toughness by 2 vs. close combat weapons with AP -.

Unstoppable: Before making a Recovery test, roll 1D6; on a 4+ the skill triggers as follows:

  • If the fighter has at least one Flesh Wound token, they may discard a single Flesh Wound token.
  • If the fighter had no Flesh Wounds, they instead gain an additional Injury die for the Recovery test, the controlling player chooses which die to discard.

Scavenged Weapons

Poor quality - Ammo checks are always failed.

  • Open crates using a Loot (basic) action (no test required).
  • If a fighter with a Scavenged weapon is Taken out of Action, the weapon is dropped at the centre of their base. Other fighters can pick it up using a Loot (basic) action.

Weapon Trait Reminders

Disarm: If the roll to hit = natural 6, the target cannot use any weapons in their Reaction attacks for the remainder of that round (they make unarmed attacks instead).

Knockback: If roll to hit >= targets' Strength, the target is pushed directly back 1".

  • If the fighter cannot be moved back 1" without hitting impassable terrain or another model they are moved as far as possible and the attacks' Damage is increased by 1.
  • If any part of the knocked back fighter's base crosses the edge of a platform, make an Initiative test. If this is failed, they will fall. If this is passed, they stop moving at the edge of the platform.

Rapid Fire: Remember that:

  • You can assign shots, after the first, onto models within 3" of the original target provided they are no harder to hit than the original target. Be nice & narrative, explain the intention to do this before the dice are rolled.
  • If you miss, resolve a Stray shot for every bullet icon rolled on the Firepower dice.
  • If rolling multiple Firepower die, two or more Ammo check results causes a Jam and that weapon cannot be used for the rest of the battle.

Parry: After an enemy makes successful close combat attacks against this fighter, you can force the attacker to re-roll one successful hit. A fighter armed with two weapons with the Parry trait, can force the attacking player to re-roll two successful hits instead (noting you cannot force a reroll of a reroll)

Power: If the roll to hit = natural 6, no save roll can be made against the attack (except Field armour save rolls) and the Damage caused is increased by 1. Attacks made by a Power weapon cannot be Parried except by other weapons with the Power trait.

Scattershot: Resolve D6 Wound rolls instead of 1.

Toxin: Instead of making a Wound roll, roll 1D6; If the result is equal to or higher than the target's Toughness, or is a natural 6, make an Injury roll for them (regardless of their Wounds characteristic).

Unwieldy: Weapons with this trait:

  • Shoot (Basic) is degraded to Shoot (Double).
  • Unwieldy ranged weapons can never be used with shooting skills (such as Fast Shot, or Hip-Shooting) even if you have suspensors or other mitigants for the Shoot (Double) effect.
  • If the weapon has both the Melee and Unwieldy weapon traits, the fighter cannot use a second close combat weapon.
